We've heard all of them. Every single one. Here's each excuse and what's actually going on.

"I'm Indian, my skin is already dark, I don't need SPF."most common

Melanin provides some natural protection — roughly SPF 13 equivalent for deeper skin tones. That's not nothing, but it's not remotely sufficient for Indian sun exposure. The UV index in most Indian cities sits between 8 and 11 for most of the year. That's "very high" to "extreme."

More importantly: darker skin tones are actually more prone to hyperpigmentation from UV damage — the melanin that protects you can also overproduce in response to sun exposure. The dark spots and uneven tone people try to treat with serums? Often caused by skipping SPF in the first place.

SPF isn't a fair skin thing. UV radiation does not check your complexion.
"It makes my face look white / ashy."valid concern, old problem

White cast comes from mineral UV filters sitting on the skin surface and reflecting light. Older sunscreens leaned heavily on these because they're cheaper.

Dawn Shield uses a hybrid filter system — chemical filters that absorb UV, with a smaller mineral component for the PA++++ UVA rating. No visible white cast, tested across Indian skin tones. If you've avoided sunscreen because of this, this specific formulation was made for you.

Why SPF 50+ and not SPF 30?

SPF 30 is technically sufficient in controlled conditions with correct application. In real world India — where most people apply 40–60% of the recommended amount, reapplication doesn't happen, and UV indices are consistently very high — SPF 50+ gives you enough buffer that even suboptimal application provides meaningful protection. It's not overcautious. It's realistic about how people actually use sunscreen.

The chemical vs. mineral debate

Mineral filters (zinc oxide, titanium dioxide) are gentler for very sensitive skin. Chemical filters have no white cast and feel lighter. We chose a hybrid system that skews chemical because the white cast from full mineral SPF is the biggest reason people skip sunscreen on Indian skin tones. If you have very reactive skin or are pregnant, full mineral is the better choice. This one is for everyone else.

You need to reapply. We'll say it.

If you're outdoors: every 2 hours. If you're indoors all day: morning application is fine. We know reapplication is the step everyone skips. We also know that one application of SPF 50+ in the morning, even without reapplication, is dramatically better than zero sunscreen. Start with the morning habit. The reapplication habit comes second.

Who should be careful with this

If you're pregnant: some chemical UV filters have limited safety data in pregnancy. Full mineral SPF is the recommended choice. If you have rosacea or extremely reactive skin: the chemical filters can occasionally cause flushing. Patch test first. If you're already on prescription retinoids: this product is even more essential — retinoids increase sun sensitivity significantly.

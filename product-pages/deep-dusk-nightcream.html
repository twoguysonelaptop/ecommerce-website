the honest part

Retinol is the most evidence backed anti aging ingredient in skincare. Dermatologists recommend it broadly. And yet most people who try it stop after two weeks.

Why people quit retinol

The retinol purge is real. Peeling, redness, sensitivity, dryness that lasts weeks. Synthetic retinol binds aggressively to skin receptors and causes dramatic cell turnover. Effective, yes. Comfortable, no. People who've tried it and quit describe the same two weeks: flaking skin, tight feeling, everything stings.

What marine bio retinol from microalgae actually is

Certain marine microalgae species naturally produce retinol equivalent compounds as part of their cellular repair mechanisms. These compounds are encapsulated in lipid structures that closely mirror skin cell membranes, allowing gradual, controlled release rather than the immediate receptor flood of synthetic retinol. The result is the same cellular outcome — accelerated cell turnover, collagen signalling, pigmentation reduction — with significantly reduced irritation. Not zero irritation. Significantly reduced.

Why we chose this over synthetic retinol

We considered synthetic retinol at very low concentrations. We chose marine bio retinol for three reasons. First: the natural lipid encapsulation is genuinely superior for controlled delivery. Second: the brightening and barrier repair properties of the algae extract are additive — they come alongside the retinol activity, not just from it. Third: marine microalgae is rapidly renewable, requires no arable land, and is harvested without petrochemical processing.

Can you use this if you've never used retinol before?

Yes — and marine bio retinol is a better starting point than synthetic retinol for first timers. Start every other night for the first two weeks. If your skin tolerates it with no sensitivity, move to every night. If you experience slight dryness in the first week, that's normal adaptation. Don't stop.

The sunscreen dependency — we'll say it clearly

Retinol — even bio marine retinol — increases photosensitivity. The newer skin cells being produced are more vulnerable to UV damage. Using this without SPF 50+ during the day means you are actively undoing some of what Deep Dusk accomplished overnight. Products 03 and 04 are in a relationship. Both need to be present for either to work properly.

Who should be cautious

Pregnant or breastfeeding: avoid retinol in all forms, bio marine included. If you have rosacea: start very slowly, every third night, and monitor. Active eczema or compromised barrier: heal the barrier first, introduce this after. When in doubt, ask a dermatologist. We're a night cream, not medical advice.

Because it's a night cream. It goes on at night. It's a cream. Marketing names for skincare products have gotten so detached from what things actually are that we found it exhausting.

A face wash is a rinse-off product. 60 seconds of contact time. These actives are calibrated for that window — they're doing real work in short exposure. But the biggest changes come from consistency. Use it twice a day. Every day. That's the actual active ingredient.

Eight things doing eight jobs. No filler claiming to be actives. Tap each one.

Niacinamide 2%
Pore control and oil regulation — the foundation ingredient.
hero

Vitamin B3. At 2% in a rinse-off format, it's doing two things: regulating sebum production so your skin makes less oil over time, and supporting the skin barrier so everything else in this formula can work without irritating you. Lower concentration than the body wash (4%) because your face gets this twice a day — morning and night. The cumulative exposure adds up. No need to overdo it.

2% — calibrated for twice-daily rinse-off use. Cumulative, not aggressive.
Salicylic Acid 0.5%
Gets inside the pore. That's the whole point.
hero

BHA — beta hydroxy acid. Oil-soluble, meaning it dissolves into the sebum inside your pores and cleans from within. Most facewashes clean the surface. This one gets into the pore and dissolves what's clogging it. At 0.5% in a daily-use format, it's effective enough to prevent breakouts but gentle enough to use morning and night without irritation. The pores stay clear. The breakouts don't get a chance to start.

0.5% — the daily-use sweet spot for face skin.
Lactic Acid 0.5% + AHA+BHA+PHA System
Triple-acid exfoliation. Three depths. Every wash.
hero

Three acids, three mechanisms. AHA (including lactic acid at 0.5%) works on the surface — dissolving bonds holding dead skin cells. BHA goes deeper into pores. PHA is the gentle one — larger molecular size, slower penetration, less irritation, plus humectant properties. Lactic acid specifically is one of the gentlest AHAs, making it appropriate for twice-daily use on face skin. Together they create layered exfoliation without physical scrubbing or micro-tears.

Lactic acid at 0.5% + triple-acid system — broad exfoliation without brute force.
Sodium Hyaluronate 0.1%
Why this facewash doesn't leave your skin tight and dry.
supporting

The low-molecular-weight form of hyaluronic acid. It actually penetrates the upper skin layers rather than sitting on the surface. Holds up to 1000x its weight in water. When you're using acids in a daily face wash, moisture retention isn't optional — it's what stops the exfoliation from becoming irritation. This is the difference between "clean face" and "clean face that still feels like skin."

0.1% — enough to counterbalance the acids. Your face stays hydrated, not stripped.

Why a tube and not a pump?

The body wash has a foaming pump because body skin needs volume coverage. Your face needs a pea-sized amount. A tube gives you better control over how much you dispense and travels better. It also means no air exposure between uses — the actives stay stable longer. The packaging follows the product, not the other way around.

Can I use this on my body?

You can, but it's not efficient. The surfactant system is gentler than the body wash because face skin is thinner and more reactive. The actives are at lower concentrations for the same reason. We make a body wash (Foam Rinse) designed specifically for body skin. Use each where it belongs.

Is it fragrance-free?

Yes. The facewash is completely fragrance-free. Face skin is the most sensitive area, and the product sits on it twice a day. We didn't see a reason to add fragrance here. The body wash has a light scent because body skin tolerates it and people won't use an unscented body wash. Different decisions for different products.

100ml — how long does it last?

A pea-sized amount per wash. Used twice daily, most people get 8–10 weeks. At ₹499, that's around ₹7–9 per day. For 8 actives at real concentrations. There are single-ingredient serums that cost more per week.

Things we'll just say directly
Why only 4?
Because that's what your skin actually needs. Everything else is optional at best, anxiety-inducing at worst. We checked the science. Twice.
When do results show?
4–6 weeks. Not overnight. Not in a week. Anyone saying otherwise is selling something else, and the something else usually costs more.
Will you add products?
We've had this debate internally four times. The answer has been no every time. Ask again next year. Answer will be the same.
Is this for sensitive skin?
Yes. Formulated for daily use on all skin types. If you're nervous, patch test first. We would too. You know your skin better than we do.
The questions
Is this really a complete routine?
+
Yes. AM: Clear First + Foam Rinse in the shower, Dawn Shield after. PM: Clear First (optional) + Deep Dusk. Your skin is covered — cleansing, hydration, sun protection, barrier repair, actives for texture and tone. We didn't cut corners. We cut unnecessary products.
I already have a skincare routine. Why would I switch?
+
You probably wouldn't switch entirely. But if you're buying for someone who doesn't have a routine — a partner, sibling, a friend still using shampoo on their face — this is where to start. The formulations are also genuinely good. The skincare-obsessed people who've tried it know.
What about eye cream / toner / essence / serum?
+
Eye cream: your night cream covers the under-eye area. Toner: not necessary if your cleanser maintains pH (ours does). Essence and mist: we genuinely cannot help you here. Serum: the actives in our products are at serum-level concentrations. We just put them where you'll actually use them.
Can I use this if I'm on tretinoin or prescription actives?
+
Talk to your dermatologist — really. Our face wash has BHA which layered on top of tret can be too much for some skin. The body wash, Dawn Shield, and Deep Dusk are generally fine. When in doubt: your derm knows your skin, we don't.

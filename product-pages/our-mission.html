Our Mission

Making skincare
less of a thing. And yes, also making the founder rich. Both.

We'll get to the money part. First, the actual mission — because a brand that buries its motives in wellness language is a brand you should probably distrust.

Part one — the real reason

Skincare should take less than 5 minutes and zero confusion.

At some point, skincare became a hobby. A personality type. A content category. An overwhelming system of steps, actives, layering rules, ingredient interactions, pH considerations, and a rotating cast of new products each claiming to be non-negotiable.

Most people don't want a skincare hobby. They want their skin to be fine. They want to wash their face, put some things on it, and go live their actual life. They don't want to spend 40 minutes researching whether niacinamide and vitamin C can be used together — they can, it's fine, the science settled this years ago, but skincare content keeps the debate alive because debate generates clicks.

ODD Care Co. exists for those people. Four products. A routine you can memorise in one read. Actives at doses that actually work. And nothing else, because nothing else was necessary.

4
Products. Not 4 to start with and more coming. Not 4 hero products and 12 supporting ones. 4 products — that is the complete catalogue. We made peace with this. You should too.

The constraint isn't a gimmick. It's the whole philosophy made product. If we can't fit something into one of the four, it doesn't belong in the routine. We've held this line through every conversation about expanding. We're still holding it.

Part two — who we built this for

The person who searched "basic skincare routine" and closed the tab.

There's a person out there — a lot of them, actually — who knows they should probably have a skincare routine, tried to figure out what that means, got three paragraphs into an article that mentioned "skin cycling" and "the moisture sandwich," and decided their skin was fine actually.

That person is our customer. Also their partner who hasn't changed their routine since college. Also the person with a 12-step routine who loves skincare but has a job now and simply doesn't have the time anymore.

The skincare beginner 4 products is a complete start. nothing missing.
The skincare avoider 3.5 minutes a day. we timed it.
The skincare obsessive, tired edition the actives are actually good. you can check.
The person gifting to someone who uses shampoo on their face the whole routine. ₹1,499. you're welcome.

We are not the brand for everyone. If you love skincare as a ritual, enjoy 14 products, and want a mist and an essence and a bi-weekly mask situation — there are hundreds of brands built exactly for you. We're not competing with them. We're the brand for the people they've been ignoring.

Part three — the part other brands delete

Yes, we also want the founder to be embarrassingly rich.

Most brand mission statements end somewhere around "make the world better through skincare." We respect the instinct. We cannot write that sentence without feeling slightly dishonest.

So here's the full version: we want to make skincare genuinely easier for a large number of people, and we want that to generate enough commercial success that the people who built this can afford to live extremely well. Both things. Simultaneously. With no apology.

We don't think these goals are in conflict. In fact, the only way to achieve the first one at scale is to achieve the second one first — because a brand that can't sustain itself can't help anyone.

What "embarrassingly rich" means
Financial independence. Not making decisions based on survival. Being able to bet on ideas that take time. Buying back time.
not: a yacht named after a moisturiser. probably.
What it doesn't mean
Charging more than the products are worth. Adding unnecessary products to increase cart value. Hiding ingredients. Making you feel bad about your skin to sell you more things.
those are the rules we set for ourselves.
How we plan to get there
Make products genuinely good enough that people who know skincare recommend them to people who don't. Repeat. Don't add a 5th product just because it would help revenue.
the plan is simple. execution is the whole thing.
Why we're telling you this
Because every other brand is telling you they exist to fix your skin out of the goodness of their hearts — and we find that unconvincing. We exist because we want this to work. For you and for us.
mutual interest is still interest.

The honest version of every brand's mission is some combination of: "we saw a problem, we think we can solve it better than what exists, and we'd like to make money doing it." We just wrote it down. This is not cynicism. This is what building a company actually is.

Part four — the founder, briefly
O
The Founder
started ODD Care Co. because they were tired of both skincare complexity and dishonest brand mission statements

I have a fairly short patience for things that are more complicated than they need to be. Skincare was one of those things. I kept trying to find a routine that covered the basics — actually worked, used real actives at real concentrations, didn't require an evening seminar to understand — and kept ending up with 8 products and a drawer full of things I'd bought because someone online said they were essential.

The skincare I wanted didn't exist in the form I wanted it. Four products. Multifunctional. Clear about what's in them and why. Not trying to sell me a fifth thing at checkout. So I made it.

The wealth part is real too. I'd like ODD Care Co. to be successful enough that I can spend the rest of my working life building things I believe in, without the pressure of financial instability narrowing my options. I think that's a reasonable thing to want. I also think pretending I don't want it while still trying to sell you something would be a worse version of honesty.

So: I want your skin to be in better condition with less effort. And I'd like to be well-compensated for having figured out how to do that. Both. Genuinely.

Part five — the actual commitments

Things we'll do. In writing. On the internet.

Because a mission without specifics is just feelings.

01
Never add a product to sell more product
If we ever add a 5th product it will be because we found something genuinely not covered by the existing four and can prove it. We will explain exactly why, publicly, before we launch it.
we've had this internal debate 4 times. the answer has been no every time.
02
List every active at its actual concentration
No "contains niacinamide" without telling you it's at 2%, not 0.1%. The concentration is the whole point. We will not use ingredients decoratively for label appeal.
this is harder than it sounds. we did it anyway.
03
Keep unflattering reviews on the site
If you gave us 2 stars because the product didn't work for you, that review stays. The anonymous review saying we didn't fix their acne in a week is still on the facewash page. It will stay there.
we will also respond to it, probably.
04
Tell you when a product isn't right for you
Pregnant? We'll tell you to skip the night cream. On tretinoin? We'll tell you to go slow with the facewash. Severe KP? We'll tell you to also see a dermatologist. We'd rather lose a sale than give bad advice.
we are not dermatologists. we will keep saying this.
05
Not pretend the money doesn't matter
We are a company. We need revenue to exist. We want to be profitable. We want the founder to be financially comfortable. We will continue to say this out loud instead of hiding it behind a story about changing the world through moisturiser.
the world doesn't need changing through moisturiser. it needs a simpler routine.
